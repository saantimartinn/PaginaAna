OUR PRODUCT

Tumor cells frequently suppress anti-tumor immunity through PD-1/PD-L1 signaling, which leads to T-cell exhaustion and reduced cytotoxic activity. Our therapeutic strategy is designed to interfere with this pathway while simultaneously enhancing local immune activation within the tumor microenvironment.

To achieve this, we engineer a nanobody with high affinity for the target immune checkpoint and fuse it to IL-12, a cytokine known to promote T-cell and NK-cell activation. This modular design aims to concentrate immune stimulation at the tumor site rather than systemically.

The intended outcome is a dual-mechanism immunotherapy capable of restoring T-cell function, promoting local immune infiltration, and potentially reducing systemic toxicity compared with conventional checkpoint inhibitors.

THERAPEUTIC INDICATIONS

Our development focuses on solid tumors characterized by immune evasion and limited response to existing immunotherapies.

  • Head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (HNSCC): These tumors frequently display immunosuppressive microenvironments with high PD-L1 expression and T-cell dysfunction, making them suitable for therapies that both block immune checkpoints and enhance local cytokine signaling.

  • Malignant pleural mesothelioma (MPM): This cancer is typically resistant to current immunotherapeutic approaches due to low basal immune infiltration. A targeted cytokine-checkpoint fusion may help convert this “cold” microenvironment into an immune-responsive state.

    • By prioritizing indications with high unmet clinical need and clear immunological rationale, we aim to deliver therapeutics capable of producing meaningful clinical benefit while establishing a strong foundation for future expansion into broader oncology settings.
